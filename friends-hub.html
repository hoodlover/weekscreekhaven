Hot Tub & Spa

Soak Time: Limit to 15 minutes. Step out if you feel lightheaded.

Towels: Use the dedicated large towels in the entertainment room — not the bathroom ones.

Cover Lifter: Always use the lifter arm. Don't drag the cover off the edge.

When Done: Replace cover fully on all sides to keep heat in and critters out.

Kids: Supervised at all times. Limit to 5–10 minutes in hot water.

Alcohol & Hot Tubs: Use extra caution — heat + booze = dizzy fast.

Glassware: No glass near the hot tub. Use the plastic cups provided.

Lower-Level Spa Tub: Jets are currently out of order — tub itself is fully usable.

Mountain Safety

Black Bears: Active year-round. Never feed them. Make noise, back away slowly. No food outside.

Other Wildlife: Coyotes, deer, wild turkeys. Keep distance, don't feed.

Snakes: Watch your step on trails, wood piles, tall grass. Copperheads are present. Leave them alone.

Firepit: Never leave unattended. Douse with water, stir ash until cold. Check Fannin County burn bans.

Mountain Roads: Steep and slick after rain or ice. Take it slow, especially at night. 4WD recommended in winter.

Weather: Changes fast. Bring a layer — it's 10–15°F cooler at elevation.

Emergencies: 911 still works with spotty service. Nearest hospital: Fannin Regional, ~15 min away.

Garbage & Bear Bins

Opening: Pinch both side handles inward at the same time, then lift the lid. Takes a little force — that's the point!

Closing: Press the lid down firmly until it clicks locked on both sides.

Bag it first. Always bag your trash before placing it in any can.

Food waste & smelly trash goes in the tall bear-proof can on the front porch. Latch it completely. Bears are real.

General trash (dry, non-food) can go in the smaller deck bin.

No loose garbage outside. Ever. Bears are crafty and they hold grudges.

Full can? Extra trash bags under the kitchen sink.

A Note on Wildlife

"For your safety, please don't feed the wildlife, pet them, or leave loose garbage outside or in your car. There are black bears, coyotes, and deer in the area."

Opening the bear-proof bin requires the "pinch and lift" technique:

  • The Mechanism: Place your fingers inside the two slots on the black latch.
  • The Action: Pinch the internal tabs together while simultaneously lifting the lid with your other hand.

Not so great when you have trash in your hand!

Always use the bear-proof bin on the side of the porch!

Before You Leave...

Linens: Strip sheets from all used beds.
Laundry: Wash used sheets and towels. Transfer to dryer and start.
Dishes: Load and start the dishwasher. No dirty dishes in the sink.
Fireplace: Set to 'Pilot.' Close the screens.
Trash: Bagged and in the bear-proof cans. Food waste in the tall porch can.
Fridge: Clean out all personal items.
Hot Tub: Towels to laundry area. Close cover, lower temp to 80°F.
Bathrooms: Clean used bathrooms so they're ready for the next adventure.
Lights & Air: All lights off. Thermostat to 75°F (Summer) or 60°F (Winter).
Lock Up: Windows closed, smart doors locked. ("Alexa, going out")
