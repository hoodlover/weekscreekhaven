Hot Tub & Spa

On Arrival: Crank it to 104°F the moment you drop your bags — it takes a solid 2+ hours to get there. Future you (holding a drink at 10 PM) will be extremely grateful. On departure, set it back to 80°F for the next crew.

Soak Time: Limit to 15 minutes. Step out if you feel lightheaded.

Towels: Use the dedicated large towels in the entertainment room — not the bathroom ones.

Cover Lifter: Always use the lifter arm. Don’t drag the cover off the edge.

When Done: Replace cover fully on all sides. Keep heat in, critters out.

Kids: Supervised at all times. Limit to 5–10 minutes in hot water.

Alcohol & Hot Tubs: Heat + booze = dizzy fast. You’ve been warned.

Glassware: No glass near the hot tub. Use the plastic cups provided.

Lower Spa Tub: Jets out of order — tub itself is fully usable.

Mountain Safety

Black Bears: Active year-round. Never feed them. Make noise, back away slowly. No food outside — ever.

Other Wildlife: Coyotes, deer, wild turkeys. Keep distance, don’t feed.

Snakes: Watch your step on trails, wood piles, tall grass. Copperheads are real. Leave them alone.

Firepit: Never leave unattended. Douse with water, stir ash until cold. Check Fannin County burn bans.

Mountain Roads: Steep and slick after rain or ice. Slow down, especially at night.

Weather: Changes fast. Bring a layer — 10–15°F cooler at elevation.

Emergencies: 911 still works. Nearest hospital: Fannin Regional, ~15 min.

Bugs

Yep. Cabin in the woods. Bugs be buggin’.

This is their turf — we just have better furniture and working Wi-Fi. A little mountain attitude goes a long way out here.

Bring: Bug spray, DEET if you mean serious business, a citronella candle if you want to feel fancy while losing.

Pro tip: Just hand the whole situation to your 9-year-old. They like bugs. They’ll sort it out and probably make a new friend in the process.

Ticks: Do a quick once-over after hiking through tall grass or brush. Tweezers are in the first aid kit — use them, not your fingers.

Inside the cabin: The occasional spider or rogue moth is harmless and deeply offended by the screaming. Show some respect.

The Bug Survival Kit

●Bug spray — DEET for serious situations, lemon eucalyptus if you’re a naturalist
●Deodorant — doubles as mild deterrent for those committed to smelling good while losing
●Mountain attitude: unbothered, undefeated
●One willing 9-year-old — fearless, curious, and has nothing to lose

Garbage & Bear Bins

Opening: Pinch both side handles inward at the same time, then lift. Takes force — that’s the point.

Closing: Press down firmly until it clicks locked on both sides.

Bag it first. Always. Every time.

Food waste & smelly trash goes in the tall bear-proof can on the front porch. Bears are real and they hold grudges.

General trash (dry, non-food) can go in the smaller deck bin.

No loose garbage outside. Ever. Did we mention bears?

Full can? Extra bags under the kitchen sink.

The Sink & The Throne

Quick PSA: the septic tank is not a garbage disposal. It’s a slow-going digestive system with no sense of humor and a long memory. Be kind to it.

🍜 Kitchen Sink

If it isn’t water or soap, it belongs in the trash. The can is literally right there — they’re basically partners at this point.
