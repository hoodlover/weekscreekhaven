Hot Tub & Spa

Soak Time: Limit soaks to 15 minutes, especially at high temperatures. Step out if you feel lightheaded, overheated, or dizzy.

Towels: Use the dedicated large towels in the entertainment room — not the bathroom towels.

Cover Lifter: The photo guide on the left shows all four steps. Always use the lifter arm — don't drag the cover off the edge.

When You're Done: Replace the cover fully and make sure it's seated on all sides to keep heat in and debris out.

Children: Kids should be supervised at all times and should limit exposure to 5–10 minutes in hot water.

Alcohol & Hot Tubs: Use extra caution — heat and alcohol together increase the risk of overheating and dizziness.

Glassware: No glass in or immediately around the hot tub. Use the plastic cups provided.

Lower-Level Spa Tub: The jets in the lower-level bathroom tub are currently out of order — the tub itself is fully usable.

Mountain Safety

Black Bears: Bears are active in this area year-round. Never feed them or approach them. If you see one on the property, make noise, back away slowly, and give it space — they will move on. Do not leave food, coolers, or trash outside.

Other Wildlife: Coyotes, deer, and wild turkeys are common. Keep your distance and do not feed any wildlife — it makes them bolder and more dangerous over time.

Snakes: Watch where you step on trails, around wood piles, and in tall grass. Most are harmless, but copperheads are present in the mountains. Leave them alone and they'll leave you alone.

Firepit: Never leave a fire unattended. Douse it completely with water and stir the ash until everything is cold to the touch before walking away. Check for any active burn bans in Fannin County before lighting up.

Mountain Roads: The driveway and surrounding roads are steep and can be slick after rain or ice. Take it slow, especially at night. Four-wheel drive is strongly recommended in winter conditions.

Weather: Mountain weather can change quickly. Check the forecast before heading out on hikes and bring a layer — it's often 10–15°F cooler at elevation than in town.

Cell Service & Emergencies: Service can be spotty in the area. If you have an emergency, 911 is still reachable. The nearest hospital is Fannin Regional in Blue Ridge, about 15 minutes away.

Garbage &
Bear Bins

Opening the bear bin: Pinch both side handles inward at the same time, then lift the lid up. It takes a little force — that's the point!

Closing it: Press the lid down firmly until you hear it click locked on both sides.

Bag it first. Always bag your trash before placing it in any can.

Food waste & smelly trash goes in the tall bear-proof can on the front porch. Latch the lid completely — bears are real.

General trash (dry, non-food) can go in the smaller deck bin.

No loose garbage outside. Never leave open bags or food scraps on the deck or ground.

Full can? Extra trash bags are under the kitchen sink.

Departure Checklist

Trash: Bagged trash in outside deck bin. Food trash must go in the bear-proof tall garbage can.
Dishes: Load and start dishwasher. Please do not leave dirty dishes in the sink.
Bathrooms: Clean used bathrooms so they are ready for the next adventures.
Fireplace: Fully off. Leave pilot light on. Close the screens.
Linens: Strip beds and place in laundry area. Please start at least one load (start to finish).
Hot Tub: Towels to laundry area. Close cover and lower temp to 80°F.
Lights & Air: All lights off. Thermostat to 75°F (Summer) or 60°F (Winter).
Lock Up: Windows closed and smart doors locked. (Say "Alexa, going out").
