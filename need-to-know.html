Hot Tub & Spa

On Arrival: Crank it to 104°F the moment you drop your bags — takes 2+ hours to get there. Future you (holding a drink at 10 PM) will be very grateful. Before leaving, drop it back to 80°F for the next crew.

Soak Time: Limit soaks to 15 minutes. Step out if you feel lightheaded or overheated.

Towels: Use the dedicated large towels in the entertainment room — not the bathroom ones.

Cover Lifter: Always use the lifter arm. Don’t drag the cover off the edge.

When Done: Replace cover fully on all sides. Keeps the heat in and the critters out.

Kids: Supervised at all times. Limit to 5–10 minutes in hot water.

Alcohol & Hot Tubs: Heat + booze = dizzy fast. You’ve been warned.

Glassware: No glass near the hot tub. Use the plastic cups provided.

Lower Spa Tub: Jets out of order — the tub itself is fully usable.

Mountain Safety

Black Bears: Active year-round. Never feed them. Make noise, back away slowly, give them space. No food outside — ever.

Other Wildlife: Coyotes, deer, wild turkeys. Keep your distance and don’t feed any of them.

Snakes: Watch your step on trails, around wood piles, and in tall grass. Copperheads are present. Leave them alone and they’ll return the favor.

Firepit: Never leave unattended. Douse with water, stir ash until cold. Check Fannin County burn bans before lighting up.

Mountain Roads: Steep and slick after rain or ice. Take it slow, especially at night. 4WD recommended in winter.

Weather: Changes fast up here. Bring a layer — it’s often 10–15°F cooler than in town.

Emergencies: 911 still works. Nearest hospital: Fannin Regional, ~15 min in Blue Ridge.

Bugs

Yep. Cabin in the woods. Bugs be buggin’.

This is their turf — we just have better furniture and working Wi-Fi. A little mountain attitude goes a long way out here.

Bring: Bug spray, DEET if you mean serious business, a citronella candle if you want to feel fancy while losing.

Pro tip: Just hand the whole situation to your 9-year-old. They like bugs. They’ll sort it out and probably make a new friend in the process.

Ticks: Do a quick once-over after hiking through tall grass or brush. Tweezers are in the first aid kit — use them, not your fingers.

Inside the cabin: The occasional spider or rogue moth is harmless and deeply offended by the screaming. Show some respect.

The Bug Survival Kit

●Bug spray — DEET for serious situations, lemon eucalyptus if you’re a naturalist
●Deodorant — doubles as mild deterrent for those committed to smelling good while losing
●Mountain attitude: unbothered, undefeated
●One willing 9-year-old — fearless, curious, and has nothing to lose

Garbage &
Bear Bins

Opening: Pinch both side handles inward at the same time, then lift the lid. It takes force — that’s the whole point.

Closing: Press the lid down firmly until it clicks locked on both sides.

Bag it first. Always bag your trash before placing it in any can. Every time.

Food waste & smelly trash goes in the tall bear-proof can on the front porch. Latch it completely. Bears are real and they hold grudges.

General trash (dry, non-food) can go in the smaller deck bin.

No loose garbage outside. Ever. Did we mention the bears?

Full can? Extra bags are under the kitchen sink.

The Sink & The Throne

Quick PSA: the septic tank is not a garbage disposal. It’s a slow-going digestive system with no sense of humor and a long memory. Be kind to it.

🍜 Kitchen Sink

If it isn’t water or soap, it belongs in the trash. The can is literally right there — they’re basically partners at this point.

❌ Keep Out of the Drain

  • Food scraps & grease
  • Coffee grounds
  • Paper towels
  • Anything solid, chunky, or questionable

✓ Fair Game

  • Water
  • Dish soap & rinse water
  • Good vibes

🚽 The Throne

The toilet is on the same septic system. Treat it with the same respect. “Flushable” wipes are a marketing lie — they will absolutely not be forgiven.

❌ Do Not Flush

  • “Flushable” wipes (they’re not — they’re liars)
  • Paper towels
  • Feminine products
  • Anything that isn’t TP

✓ Throne-Approved

  • Septic-safe TP (provided — please use it)
  • Water
  • That’s genuinely it

A happy septic means a happy cabin. Your future self and the next three families are quietly counting on you.

Departure Checklist

This is not an AirBnB. Let’s leave it great for the next family visit.

Trash: Bagged trash in outside deck bin. Food trash must go in the bear-proof tall can on the porch.
Dishes: Load and start the dishwasher. Please do not leave dirty dishes in the sink. We will find out.
Bathrooms: Clean the bathrooms you used so they’re ready for the next adventure.
Fireplace: Fully off. Leave pilot light on. Close the screens.
Linens: Strip beds and place in laundry area. Start at least one load (start-to-finish).
Hot Tub: Towels to laundry area. Close cover. Lower temp to 80°F.
Lights & Air: All lights off. Thermostat to 75°F (Summer) or 60°F (Winter).
Lock Up: Windows closed and smart doors locked. (Say “Alexa, going out”)

Quick Exit Command:

“Alexa, Going Home”

Check-out is at 3:00 PM.
